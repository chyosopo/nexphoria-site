# LongevityMeds.com — Copy, Funnel & Logic Study

> Commissioned by Chiya 2026-07-06: *"I don't like the design, but the copy,
> the flow and the logic is amazing. Extract as much as you can and tell me
> what we can do for our brand."*
>
> This is a **copy/strategy** study, not a design study (Chiya explicitly does
> not want their look). Everything below is what we can learn from **how they
> sell**, filtered through Nexphoria's standing laws (institutional bank voice,
> no discount energy, true claims only, crimson reserved, two worlds).

---

## 1. What the site actually is (the whole map)

It is **tiny on purpose**. The entire public site is:

- `/` — home (weight-loss led)
- `/semaglutide/v1`, `/tirzepatide/v1` — two GLP-1 landers (weight loss)
- `/nad/v1` — NAD+ lander (longevity/energy) — clearly their **newest and most
  evolved** page
- `/login`, and ten `/legal/*` pages (HIPAA, safety, states-available,
  telehealth consent, refund, CCPA, partner info, terms, privacy)

No blog. No science library. No catalog. No "two worlds." **Three products,
one funnel, relentlessly repeated.** The discipline itself is the first lesson:
they never make the visitor choose from a shelf — they push one decision
("start your consultation") over and over.

Company: Longevity Meds LLC, 378 Grand Ave, Pawtucket RI. LegitScript-certified.
Positions on compounded 503A GLP-1s + NAD+ — the exact regulatory lane
Nexphoria lives in.

## 2. The positioning line (steal the *structure*, not the words)

**"Lose The Weight, Live Longer."**

Weight loss is the **front door** (proven demand, easy to sell); longevity is
the **halo** that upgrades a commodity GLP-1 purchase into an identity. NAD+ is
the longevity flanker product that makes "Longevity" in the name true.

> **For Nexphoria:** we already have the reverse — we lead with *longevity/
> performance* and treat weight as one goal among many. Their lesson is the
> value of a **single spearhead benefit** in the masthead. Our "A protocol.
> Not a purchase." is a category line, not a benefit line. Consider a
> benefit-forward companion in the hero for each world (his: measurable
> strength; hers: how you want to feel) — which we've started with the gate
> straplines. Keep pushing that down onto the home hero.

## 3. Pricing psychology — the part worth studying closely

Four moves, stacked:

1. **Strikethrough anchor:** "Starting at ~~164~~ **49** / month." A big
   first-month discount anchor.
   → **Do NOT copy.** This is discount-brand energy and violates our
   institutional-bank voice law. It's the single most off-brand thing on their
   site. Noted here so we consciously reject it.

2. **"All Dosages, One Transparent Price."** Every dose (2.5–15 mg) costs the
   same. This kills the "the dose I need will cost more" anxiety before it
   forms. **Strongly worth adopting** — a real trust move, on-brand.

3. **Daily-cost reframe:** "$6.50 per day." Makes a $195/mo number feel
   trivial. On-brand if framed calmly ("less than a daily coffee" is too cute
   for us — but a plain per-day figure is fine).

4. **Commitment ladder:** Supply duration selector — Monthly / 3-Month /
   6-Month / 12-Month, with the 12-month showing an annual total ($1,973.88).
   Raises LTV and frames the annual number as the "real" commitment.
   → We already have cadence pricing (1mo/3mo/6mo) in BuyBox. Their lesson:
   **surface the multi-month plan as a first-class selector, not a discount
   footnote**, and show the honest total.

**Bundle framing (best line):** NAD+ = *"Starts at $199/Month — includes
consultation, medication, and delivery."* One number, everything in it. We
should say this explicitly at every price: **what the number includes** (labs,
physician review, medication, shipping) so nobody fears a surprise fee. This
pairs perfectly with our existing `PrescribedPromise` ("no charge unless a
physician prescribes").

## 4. CTA language — the NAD page cracked something

The two older weight-loss pages use hard-commerce CTAs: **"Get Started,"
"Buy Now," "Add to Cart."**

The newest page (NAD) replaced them all with:

> **"Check If You Qualify" — "Takes less than 2 minutes."**

This is softer, lower-commitment, and quietly implies **screening/exclusivity**
(not everyone qualifies → this is real medicine, not a checkout). It's repeated
**six-plus times** straight down the page, always with the 2-minute reassurance.

> **For Nexphoria:** this is almost exactly our "Check eligibility" gated CTA —
> validation that our instinct is right. The learnings to apply:
> - Pair every eligibility CTA with a **time cost** ("Takes about two minutes")
>   — it's the highest-leverage micro-copy on their site.
> - Use the **qualify** frame as the *primary* verb on medical pages and keep
>   "Add to protocol" for the shelf/PDP. We already split these; tighten the
>   consistency so the softer verb leads on Science/Bloodwork/How-It-Works.

## 5. The objection-handling comparison table (their single best asset)

On the NAD page: **"Not all NAD+ is created equal."** A three-column table:

| | **Prescription NAD+ (theirs)** | Oral supplements | Unregulated peptide suppliers |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dosage | 500–1000 mg, clinically dosed | Low-dose, variable | Unknown, often unlabelled |
| Approved for use | Prescribed by licensed providers | OTC, unregulated | Not clinically regulated |
| Sourcing | Pharmacy-grade, verified | Variable | Frequently unverified, no COA |
| Absorption | High (injection) | Poor (degraded in digestion) | Inconsistent |
| Oversight | Consultation & guidance | None | None |
| Results | Consistent over time | Minimal evidence | Unpredictable / unsafe |
| Cost | From $199/mo | $30–60, lower efficacy | Variable, hidden risks |

This positions them as the **safe middle** between "weak OTC supplements" and
"dangerous gray-market peptides." It converts the customer's biggest fear
(*is buying peptides online sketchy?*) into the reason to buy from them.

> **For Nexphoria — highest-value single takeaway.** We should build our own
> version: **"Not all peptides are created equal"** — Nexphoria
> (physician-prescribed, 503A-compounded, batch-tested, lab-gated) vs.
> **research-chemical "not for human use" vendors** vs. **OTC supplement
> knock-offs.** Every row we can fill *truthfully* — COA/testing, physician
> oversight, lab gating, sourcing. This is on-brand (it's about rigor, not
> hype), it's true, and it neutralizes the exact objection our category faces.
> Recommended as a new section on Science and/or the PDPs.

## 6. Symptom laddering — meet them at the feeling, not the molecule

Homepage lists clickable symptom pills: *Persistent fatigue · Emotional stress ·
Increased appetite · Compromised mobility · Low mood/anxiety · Weight regulation ·
Low self-esteem · Sleep disturbances.*

They enter through **how the customer feels**, then route to a molecule. This is
the same instinct behind our per-world feeling lines (`CATEGORY_FEELING` /
`CATEGORY_FEELING_WOMEN`) and goal tiles — validation we're on the right path.
Their lesson: make the **symptom the clickable entry object**, so the visitor
self-selects into a goal before they ever see a compound name. We do this on the
goal tiles; consider a symptom-first band on each world home.

## 7. The best pure copywriting on the site (NAD identity framing)

> *"Most of them didn't start because they were unwell. They started because
> they were tired of performing at 70% and suspected their body was capable of
> more."*

This is **aspirational identity**, not sickness. It sells to the high-functioning
person who wants the last 30%, which is exactly Nexphoria's buyer. It's calm,
confident, and completely on-brand for our voice.

Other strong lines to learn the *cadence* from (not copy verbatim):
- *"Restore Energy, Focus, and Recovery — at the Cellular Level."* (benefit,
  then mechanism, in one breath)
- *"NAD+ isn't a supplement trend, it's a coenzyme your body relies on."*
  (reframes category away from wellness fluff toward biology — our exact move)
- *"Not crash diets. Not guesswork. Just clinician-guided medical support."*
  (negation → negation → the calm alternative — a clean rhetorical pattern)

> **For Nexphoria:** the "performing at 70%, capable of more" frame is worth an
> adaptation per world. His: *"Not broken. Just running below spec."* Hers:
> *"You're not failing. Your biology changed — and it can be read."* (drafts —
> for Chiya's voice approval, not auto-shipped.)

## 8. Testimonials — the format is smart, but we can't copy the content

The NAD reviews are labeled **"Community Review"** (not "verified patient"),
first-person, and deliberately **underwhelming and honest**:

> *"I didn't notice much the first few days, honestly. But around the second
> week I realized my usual afternoon crash wasn't hitting as hard."*
> *"My first order took an extra day or two longer than it said, which was kinda
> annoying. But when I messaged them they replied quick."*

Realistic, slightly-negative reviews read as **far more credible** than glowing
ones. The "Community Review" label and "Results may vary" are also legal
softeners.

> **For Nexphoria — learn the format, not the fabrication.** Our law forbids
> invented testimonials, and these are almost certainly synthetic. The takeaway
> is the *tone* to aim for **once we have real ones** (blocked on Chiya): honest,
> specific, unpolished, week-by-week, and not uniformly positive. Do not ship
> any until real, consented reviews exist. This reinforces our existing
> 8.5-blocked item.

## 9. Compliance discipline — directly relevant to our FDA caution

This is a compounded-drug seller doing the hedging **correctly**, which matters
because it's the same tightrope that made us retire the "Peptide Pharmacy"
lockup:

- **Hedged verbs everywhere:** "*may* support," "*may* help," "designed to,"
  "some patients report," "clinician-supervised" — never "will," never "cures."
- **Explicit compounded-drug disclaimer** on every commercial surface:
  *"Compounded drugs are not FDA-approved and have not been evaluated by the FDA
  … No representation is made that these are equivalent to any FDA-approved
  product."*
- **A full black-box safety page** (thyroid C-cell tumor warning, contra-
  indications, BMI indications) — genuinely rigorous, not a token page.
- **LegitScript certification** displayed with a plain-language explanation.
- **Honest cancellation terms:** "cancel anytime — pro-rated refund, $50
  cancellation fee." They state the fee openly; transparency reads as trust.

> **For Nexphoria:** audit our copy for any un-hedged efficacy claim and adopt
> the same grammar. Our Science page already labels evidence tiers honestly
> (we just fixed the hero grades to match) — this is the same instinct, and we
> should extend the hedged-verb discipline to every outcome statement sitewide.
> A dedicated Safety page at their level of rigor is worth building.

## 10. Structural / funnel patterns worth adopting

- **Three-step process, repeated on every page:** Online Assessment → Clinician
  Review → Treatment Delivered (NAD adds a 4th: ongoing support & monitoring).
  Same three words, every page. We have this; keep it **verbatim-consistent**
  across pages rather than re-worded per page.
- **"Currently In Stock. Orders Ship in 72 Hours."** — availability reassurance
  doubling as gentle urgency. On-brand if stated as fact, not countdown theater.
- **Trust ribbon repeated down the page:** "Trusted by 100,000+ patients ·
  Rated 4.8/5 · LegitScript certified · Pharmacy-grade." We must only use
  **numbers we can defend** — no invented patient counts or star ratings
  (that's their weakest, least-defensible claim). Use real proof (503A testing,
  physician oversight, lab-gating) instead.
- **What's-included restated at every CTA** so the price never feels like the
  start of a series of fees.

## 11. What to deliberately REJECT

1. **The design** — Chiya's call; already excluded.
2. **The ~~$164~~ $49 discount anchor** — discount-brand energy, violates our
   voice law. This is their most off-brand move.
3. **Synthetic testimonials / unverifiable "100,000+ patients / 4.8★"** — we
   ship true claims only. Learn the format; source the substance.
4. **Single-vertical flatness** — their simplicity is a strength for a 3-SKU
   brand, but our depth (two worlds, science library, catalog) is a moat we
   shouldn't flatten. Borrow their *funnel focus per page*, not their catalog
   thinness.
